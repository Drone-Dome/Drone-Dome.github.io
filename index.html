INTEGRATED SOLUTION

Dual-Layer Autonomous Defense System

Our comprehensive Drone Dome ecosystem combines 200 VTOL surveillance drones with 500 heavy-lift suppression UAVs, creating a continuous wildfire prevention mesh that reduces detection-to-response time from hours to minutes.

Surveillance Layer

200 VTOL quadplane drones with thermal IR and RGB cameras, AI-powered wildfire detection, 150-mile range, covering ~4,000 acres per trip with 30-minute continuous revisit capability across California's 100M forested acres.

Suppression Layer

500 heavy VTOL UAVs carrying 100-200L payloads, capable of suppressing 0.1-0.2 acre incipient fires. Coordinated autonomous response with 5-8 minute turnaround time, preventing escalation of ~70% of small ignitions.

Solar Station Network

280 strategically placed solar-powered charging stations (30 surveillance + 250 suppression) across California, providing autonomous operation with minimal human oversight and optimal coverage every ~50 miles.

DEPLOYMENT ROADMAP

Phased Implementation Strategy

Strategic rollout plan designed to validate technology, scale operations, and achieve full statewide coverage while integrating with existing Cal Fire and FAA systems.

Phase 1: Year 0-2

Pilot Program

Deploy and test 10 surveillance drones + 10 suppression drones + 5 charging stations in one high-risk county (Butte or San Bernardino). Validate AI detection algorithms and autonomous suppression capabilities.

Phase 2: Year 2-4

Regional Scale-Up

Expand to 50 surveillance drones + 25 suppression UAVs across 3 high-risk counties. Secure FAA waivers for BVLOS operations and integrate with existing emergency response systems.

Phase 3: Year 4-5

Statewide Deployment

Complete rollout of all 200 surveillance + 500 suppression UAVs across California. Full integration with Cal Fire dispatch systems and real-time coordination with manned aircraft operations.

Phase 4: Year 5+

Operational Excellence

Achieve full autonomous wildfire shield capability, preventing 2,000-4,000 small fires annually from becoming major wildfires, potentially saving $5-10B in avoided damages per year.
